# Fulcrum International Brand Voice Guide

## Core Identity
Fulcrum International is an impact venture studio and NGO consulting practice. We help mission-driven leaders find their bearing so their organizations can deliver the impact they were built for.

We work with nonprofit executive directors, chiefs of staff, and senior program leaders running $1M to $20M organizations. They are vision-rich and infrastructure-poor. They have a mission they believe in and a plan on paper. The plan is not moving.

## Voice Characteristics

### Dinner-Table Direct
- Lead with the real thing. No warm-up paragraphs.
- Sixth-grade clarity. Not academic, not corporate, not ministry-speak.
- Say what you mean in the fewest words possible.
- If a stat exists, use it. Real numbers beat vague claims.

### Inspirational with Authority
- Someone who has done the thing.
- Confidence without condescension.
- Empathy without hand-holding.

### Name the Pattern
- Most nonprofit content treats symptoms (fundraising tactics, board checklists).
- We go one level deeper, to the operational and strategic infrastructure that CAUSE those symptoms.
- Articles are about the reader's PATTERNS, not Fulcrum's SERVICES.
- Name the pain. Explain why it is actually happening (the thing they cannot see). Give enough clarity to take one step.

## Writing Style
- Concrete over abstract. Stories over theories.
- Short paragraphs (2 to 4 sentences max).
- Headers that say something real. Never "Introduction" or "Overview."
- Use analogies from the real world, not jargon.

## The Fulcrum Approach, Public-Facing Stage Names
Use these on the website and in articles. Never use internal framework terms outside of PDF deliverables.

| Stage | Public label | Internal term | What the leader needs |
|-------|--------------|---------------|----------------------|
| 01 | CLARITY | FOG | See where you actually stand |
| 02 | LEVERAGE | ELEVATION | Know which 2 to 3 moves matter most |
| 03 | DIRECTION | MAP | A plan connected to reality with ownership |
| 04 | EXECUTION | EXECUTION | Stay in it when reality pushes back |
| 05 | MOMENTUM | ITERATION | Systems that sustain without the founder holding everything |

Structural concept: "Four forces shaping your organization" (not "Four Concentric Levels"). Framework name on the site: "The Fulcrum Approach" (not "Bearing Flywheel").

## Positioning Phrases to Use Consistently
- "Find their bearing" (not "close the gap")
- "What you can't see" or "what leaders can't see" (the curiosity driver)
- "Deliver the impact they were built for" (the org already has purpose, just needs unlocking)
- "Mission-driven leaders" (address the leader, not the organization)

## Words We Use
- Clarity, leverage, direction, execution, momentum (the five stages)
- Bearing, pattern, infrastructure, systems, underneath
- "Your organization," "your plan," "your team" (possessive, it belongs to the leader)
- Capacity, load, friction, stall, drift

## Words We NEVER Use
- Leverage (as a verb, the noun stage name is fine), synergy, game-changer, seamless
- "In today's landscape," "In the ever-evolving"
- Disrupt, pivot, paradigm shift
- Holistic, scalable, actionable, impactful, empower
- Stakeholder (use "board," "funder," "team," or the specific role)
- Any sentence starting with "As a nonprofit leader..."
- Emojis in body copy
- Em dashes. Ever. Use commas, periods, parentheses, or colons.

## CTA Style
- Action-oriented, specific.
- "See what The Fulcrum Approach looks like for your organization" beats "Learn more"
- "Start with a Bearing Diagnostic" beats "Get started"

## Leader-First Framing
- The mission-driven leader is ALWAYS the hero. Fulcrum is the guide.
- Never "Fulcrum does X." Instead: "You see the pattern. We help name what's underneath it."
- Speak to their ambition, not their fear.

## Tone by Content Type
- Pillar pages: authoritative, comprehensive, slightly more formal. These are the definitive guides to each Bearing Framework stage.
- Blog posts: conversational, tactical, pattern-naming. Real scenarios.
- Sub-pillar pages: focused, practical, solution-oriented within one stage.

## The Funnel
Article to Free Assessment or Discovery Call to Bearing Diagnostic ($750) to Engagement. The Diagnostic is a second-date product, not a front-door entry point. Articles should build trust, not sell the Diagnostic directly.
